Travels

Australia

I lived in Australia for 1 year and 3 months, and it truly felt like a land of adventure. The country boasts diverse landscapes, from stunning beaches to rugged outback. My longest stay was in Cairns, which remains my favorite place in the world. Cairns is renowned for its relaxed atmosphere, vibrant activities, and beautiful, consistently warm weather. While Cairns offers endless opportunities for exploration, having a car is essential, and it’s worth noting that hurricanes can occasionally affect the region. Overall, Australia’s unique charm and natural beauty made my time there unforgettable.

Recommendations:

  • Snorkel or dive in the Great Barrier Reef.
  • Have some offroad adventures.
  • Visit the Blue Mountains for stunning natural scenery.
  • Experience the Outback and the iconic sunrise at Uluru.
  • Enjoy the vibrant arts and food scene in Melbourne.
  • Surf at Bondi Beach, the Gold Coast or my favorite Byron Bay.
  • Travel along the Great Ocean Road.
  • Visit Kangaroo Island for unique wildlife encounters.
  • Explore Tasmania’s pristine wilderness.
  • Discover the Daintree Rainforest, one of the oldest rainforests in the world.

New Zealand

I lived in New Zealand for 10 months, and it quickly became one of my favorite places. The country is incredibly beautiful, offering a stunning landscape of mountains, lakes, and beaches. It's a perfect destination if you love hiking, with numerous trails that showcase the breathtaking scenery. Whether exploring the lush forests or traversing the rugged terrain, New Zealand’s natural beauty makes every hike a memorable adventure.

Recommendations:

  • Take a road trip through the South Island, especially Fiordland and Queenstown.
  • Visit Hobbiton, the famous movie set from The Lord of the Rings.
  • Hike the Tongariro Alpine Crossing, one of the best day hikes in the world.
  • Explore the Waitomo Caves and see the glowworms.
  • Experience Maori culture in Rotorua.
  • Kayak in the Abel Tasman National Park.
  • Visit the geothermal wonders of Rotorua and Taupo.
  • A 3 day hike on the Queen Charlotte Track
  • Take a scenic flight over the Southern Alps and Milford Sound.
  • Enjoy the vibrant cultural scene in Wellington, New Zealand’s capital.

Japan

I’ve been living in Japan for a little over five years. I first experienced a profound sense of freedom here in 2016, and it’s been a place of immense personal growth ever since. Japan is a country of contrasts, where modernity coexists with deep-rooted traditions. Its beautiful natural landscapes are complemented by stunning old structures such as temples, shrines, and castles. Living here has been a journey of discovery and love, as I found my fiancée and built a life together in this fascinating land.

Recommendations:

  • Visit Kyoto for its beautiful temples and gardens, including Kinkaku-ji and Fushimi Inari Taisha.
  • Explore the bustling streets of Tokyo, from Shibuya Crossing to Akihabara.
  • Experience the tranquility of Mount Fuji and its surrounding lakes.
  • Attend a traditional tea ceremony in Kyoto.
  • Visit Hiroshima and learn about its history at the Peace Memorial Park.
  • Explore Nara and feed the friendly deer in Nara Park.
  • Experience the cherry blossoms in spring, especially at Ueno Park and Yoshinoyama.
  • Visit the island of Okinawa for its unique culture and beautiful beaches.
  • Take a bullet train (Shinkansen) ride to experience Japan’s high-speed rail.
  • Enjoy Japanese cuisine, from sushi in Tsukiji to street food in Osaka.
